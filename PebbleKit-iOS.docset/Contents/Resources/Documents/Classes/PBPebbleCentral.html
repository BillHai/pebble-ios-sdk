  appUUID

The UUID is used as the identifier of the watch application and is used to make sure that appMessage and dataLogging communications arrives at its companion app on the other device (and not in another app). For most app message methods there is are two variants: one that does not take a UUID parameter and one that does take a UUID parameter (for example, -appMessagesAddReceiveUpdateHandler: vs appMessagesAddReceiveUpdateHandler:withUUID:). The methods that do not take a UUID, will use the UUID as set prior to this property.

@property (nonatomic, copy) NSUUID *appUUID

Parameters

uuid

The 16 byte UUID of your app.

Discussion

The UUID is used as the identifier of the watch application and is used to make sure that appMessage and dataLogging communications arrives at its companion app on the other device (and not in another app). For most app message methods there is are two variants: one that does not take a UUID parameter and one that does take a UUID parameter (for example, -appMessagesAddReceiveUpdateHandler: vs appMessagesAddReceiveUpdateHandler:withUUID:). The methods that do not take a UUID, will use the UUID as set prior to this property.

Note: The UUID needs to be set before using either app message or data logging.
